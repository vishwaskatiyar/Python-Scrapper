According to the study conducted by the University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus in the USA and published in Science Daily on November 16, demands on healthcare institutions witheye disordersare increasing in parallel with the increase inparticulate matter(PM) caused byair pollutionin the atmosphere.

The researchers examined daily PM concentrations in the Denver metropolitan area and eye irritation and allergic disorders. As part of the study, the researchers calculatedPM10(diameter of 10 micrometers or less) andPM2.5(diameter of 2.5 micrometers or less) and examined in detail 144 thousand 313 visits to healthcare institutions due to irritation to eyes and allergies. ​​​​​​​

As a result, half of the complaints related to dry eye syndrome and conjunctivitis, also known as red eye disease, which can develop due to environmental factors such as temperature, humidity and air pollution, made up 1/3 of the complaints.

The study concluded that when the daily concentration of PM10, which should be 45 micrograms per cubic meter, reached 110, according to the criteria established by the World Health Organization (WHO), the number of patients was 2.2 times higher than the average.
