Revolutionary implant helps fight age-related vision loss.

Paragraphs:

Each week there is an announcement about a scientific breakthrough related to implants. This time, it is people of a certain age who are affected by vision problems due to macular degeneration who are concerned. Science Corporation, the company behind the Prima Brain Implant (BCI), has revealed preliminary results of its solution following a clinical trial in 38 patients from five countries with geographic atrophy.

Results obtained 6 and 12 months after the system was installed showed that the implant restored “shape vision” in patients who had lost their central visual field. Concretely, this system allows them to perform high-speed tasks, such as reading or facial recognition. After receiving the Prima implant, patients were able to read with significant improvement, including regaining the ability to recognize letters.

“For the first time, it was possible to restore true form vision to retinas damaged by age-related macular degeneration.“, said Professor Frank Holz, MD, scientific coordinator of the PRIMAvera study.”Previously, there was no real treatment to improve the vision of these patients.“, added Professor Holz, Chairman of the Department of Ophthalmology at the University of Bonn in Germany.

It’s a real turning point in the field for Max Hodak, head of Science Corporation, who says he’s excited to bring the technology to market in the coming years. This study also aims to prove its safety.

The Prima System consists of several elements beyond a surgically integrated photovoltaic implant behind the retina. A special pair of glasses, as well as a pocket processor are required along with the camera and projection system.
