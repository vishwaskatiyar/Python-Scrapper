Below are the main headlines from this edition.

NICE recommends new treatment for wet AMDA new treatment option for patients with wet AMD is set to become available on the NHS in England.

Can supplements slow the progression of late-stage AMD?New research shows that daily supplements can slow the progression of late-stage dry AMD. Dr Tiarnan Keenan from the National Eye Institute will join us to discuss these findings in our November webinar. Join us to learn more about this promising discovery and have your questions answered.

“Don’t let the diagnosis rule your life”Dawn, a yoga instructor with Stargardt disease, shares how the practice has helped her cope, offering insights she believes could benefit everyone.

Half of UK adults expect vision loss within a decade, yet many skipping eye testsA recent survey of over 2,000 UK adults found that half of people expect to experience some form of sight loss within the next decade, yet more than a quarter do not have regular eye tests.

First hearing and sight loss survey launchedThe first survey in the UK to collect data on vision and hearing loss has been launched. The UK National Eye Health and Hearing Study aims to record data to inform the NHS and policymakers and is a collaboration between sensory loss charities, Anglia Ruskin University (ARU), eye and hearing care professionals and the public sector.

“Fundraising for research is a really integral part of what we should be doing”An ophthalmology team swapped the clinic for the great outdoors, battling the dark, wind and rain in their efforts to raise vital funds for research to find a cure for macular disease. Professor Richard Gale was among the group of colleagues in Grimsby who completed the Three Peaks Challenge.

Help us understand what it’s like having eye injectionsAre you currently receiving anti-VEGF injections, or have you had injections in the last five years? We’d be so thankful if you could share your experience in our survey. We’re hoping to gain a better understanding of how treatment is given and your experiences.
